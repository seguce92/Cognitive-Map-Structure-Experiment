Instructions

This 3D virtual reality experiment aims to find out how humans structure representations of space.

You will see a simple 3D environment, and instructions on the right - something like this:

You will have to:

  1. Walk around and look around in the environment, using the keys* W, A, S, D and your mouse, and memorize the positions, and names, of the buildings. In some cases you will also have to pick up a package from one building (stand near it and press E), and deliver it to another (stand near it and press E). 
  2. Press enter when you have memorized the positions (and delivered the package if asked to). The environment will vanish.
  3. Create a map of where you remember the buildings by dragging them into their correct place with your mouse.
  4. Recall the names of all buildings you have seen a few times, starting with different buildings. It is sufficient to remember and recall the first word of the building names - for example, if you see "John's house" and "Clothes shop", you can just enter "John" and "Clothes".

There will be two experiments, with experiment 1 containing 15 and experiment 2 containing 5 tasks, for a total of 20 tasks. Each task should take about 3 minutes (~1 minute for memorization and delivery, and ~2 minutes for recalling the map and the names of the buildings). Thus, the experiment will take approx. 1 - 2 hours, depending on the number of breaks and mistakes made. (You can take breaks any time you wish).

*W to move forward, A to move left, S to move right, D to move back. Move the mouse to change where you look, like in a first-person computer game. Press Esc at any time to pause the experiment. If you don't see any buildings, press Esc and then click the "Reset current task" button. If you would like to move faster, press Shift.

Participant Information Sheet
You are being invited to take part in a research study. Before you decide it is important for you to
understand why the research is being done and what it will involve. Please take time to read the
following information carefully and discuss it with others if you wish. Please ask if there is
anything that is not clear or if you would like more information. Take time to decide whether or not
you wish to take part. Thank you for reading this.
Who will conduct the research?
MSc Tamas Madl
Title of the research.
Factors influencing the structure of spatial representations
Why have I been chosen?
Voluntary participants have been recruited via Amazon Mechanical Turk. No reason for non-participation is required.
What would I be asked to do if I take part?
This study is part of a PhD project and will help understand how humans remember maps of
environments. It will involve navigating 3D virtual environments on your computer (in your
browser), playing delivery games, creating maps, and recalling buildings. It will consist of two
experiments and take about an hour in total. Sitting in front of a computer for too long can
cause minor discomfort; but you can take a break at any time you wish.
What happens to the data collected?
The data will help understand which properties are important for structuring spatial maps of
environments, and how important they are relative to each other. All data will be used and stored
in accordance with University policy.
How is confidentiality maintained?
Confidentiality will be maintained by Amazon Mechanical Turk. Participant data will be anonymous.
What is the duration of the research?
Approximately 45 minutes, without breaks. 
Where will the research be conducted?
Anywhere participants wish; on their own computer, in their browser.
What if I change my mind?
Participants are free to leave the study at any time, without giving a reason.
Will the outcomes of the research be published?
The results of this research will be published in a PhD thesis and, if possible, in an academic journal.
